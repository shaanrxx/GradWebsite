Masep

Massep is a language spoken by the Massep people, an indigenous group in Papua Province, Indonesia. It is classified as a Papuan language and is spoken in the Mamberamo Raya Regency. Like many languages in the region, Massep faces the threat of endangerment due to factors such as limited intergenerational transmission, cultural assimilation, and the influence of dominant languages. Efforts are being made by linguists, community members, and organizations to document and revitalize Massep, preserving its linguistic diversity and cultural heritage.
